CVPR Demo 2025

FPGA-Powered milliWatt-Scale Snapshot Depth Camera

I am currently part of the BiV lab headed by Professor Emma Alexander at Northwestern University (see https://www.alexander.vision/). We travelled to the Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) in early June 2025 and demonstrated our low powered FPGA implementation of the DfDD technique. As you can see in the setup, we have to two image sensors recieving the same image from the beam splitter, but at slightly different focus levels, and feeding them to the development board via the ribbon cables. In order to align the images, we first used bilinear interpolation. Then through a series of convolutions and finally a division, it produces a depth map which is then communicated via an FT232H chip. This is all pipelined and accomplished via stream processing which allows us to buffer way fewer lines, meaning we don't need dedicated SDRAM which would consume a lot of power and can instead rely on the FPGA's embedded memory. I developed the RTL code to perform all these tasks on the FPGA (ECP5). The RTL code for the Bilinear Interpolator and Convolution Filter can be found in my Image Processing SV RTL repository. In the background picture, I am on the left and on the right is John Mamish, who is the first author on this demonstration. Soon a publication will be made which I will post a link here.
